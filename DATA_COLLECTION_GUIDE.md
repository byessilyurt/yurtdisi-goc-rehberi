# Emigration Data Collection Guide

## Purpose
This guide outlines all the detailed, official information needed to create a comprehensive emigration resource for Turkish citizens. The goal is to provide accurate, actionable information that covers the entire emigration journey from initial research to full settlement.

## Data Categories Overview

### 1. VISA & IMMIGRATION PROCESSES (CRITICAL)

#### What to Collect:
- **Official eligibility criteria** - exact requirements from government sources
- **Complete step-by-step process** - from application to approval
- **Required documents** - full list with specifications (size, format, validity)
- **Processing times** - minimum, maximum, and average (with dates)
- **Costs** - every fee involved, who pays, payment methods
- **Success rates** - approval percentages, quota information
- **Common rejection reasons** - why applications fail
- **Appeals process** - if available, how it works

#### Why It Matters:
Turkish citizens need to know EXACTLY what they need to qualify, what documents to prepare, how long it will take, and what it will cost. Missing one document or misunderstanding one requirement can lead to rejection and lost money.

#### Sources to Use:
- Official immigration department websites (USCIS, BAMF, INIS, etc.)
- Embassy websites
- Government gazette/legal publications
- Official statistical reports
- Immigration lawyer associations (for statistics)

### 2. DOCUMENT REQUIREMENTS (CRITICAL)

- **Notarization required?** (yes/no)

#### Why It Matters:
Document preparation is often the most time-consuming and error-prone part. Clear guidance on exact requirements prevents delays and rejections.

#### Special Focus:
- Turkish document apostille process
- Certified translation requirements
- Degree equivalency evaluations
- Police certificates from Turkey

## Data Quality Standards

### CRITICAL Rules:

1. **Source Everything**: Every fact needs a source URL and "last verified" date
2. **Official Sources First**: Government sites > Embassy sites > Official statistics > Legal documents > Verified guides
3. **Current Information**: Prioritize 2024-2025 data. Flag anything older than 1 year.
4. **Exact Quotes**: Use exact official terminology, especially for visa types and legal requirements
5. **Mark Uncertainties**: If conflicting info found or something unclear, mark as "NEEDS_VERIFICATION" with notes
6. **Currency Conversions**: Include original currency AND TRY equivalent with exchange rate and date
7. **No Assumptions**: If you don't find official info, mark field as "NOT FOUND - needs further research" rather than guessing
8. **Cross-Reference**: Verify critical information (costs, timelines, requirements) from 2+ sources
9. **Note Changes**: If requirements changed recently or will change soon, note both old and new with dates

### Information Hierarchy:

**Tier 1 - Most Critical (Must be 100% accurate):**
- Visa eligibility requirements
- Required documents
- Costs
- Processing times
- Legal obligations
- Rejection reasons

**Tier 2 - Very Important (Should be accurate):**
- Settlement procedures
- Healthcare system
- Housing market
- Banking
- Work rights
- Path to PR/citizenship

**Tier 3 - Important (Helpful to have accurate):**
- Cost of living details
- Education system
- Transportation
- Social integration
- Community info

**Tier 4 - Nice to Have (General guidance okay):**
- Cultural tips
- App recommendations
- Community experiences
- Personal advice

- Approval rates

### 4. Find Practical Information
- Expat forums (for experiences, not legal advice)
- Turkish community groups
- Real estate sites
- Bank websites

### 5. Cross-Reference Everything
- If two official sources conflict, note both and research further
- Check dates - immigration law changes frequently
- Verify costs in local currency on official sites
